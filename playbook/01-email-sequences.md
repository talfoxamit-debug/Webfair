# Cold Email Sequences — Fence & Exterior Contractors

**Golden rules**
- Plain text only. No images, no logos, no HTML, no links in email #1 (links tank deliverability on a cold inbox).
- One idea per email. Short. Written like a text from a busy human.
- Send from a **secondary domain** (e.g. `getstackwrk.com`), never your main. Warm it 2–3 weeks first.
- Max ~30–40 new cold emails/day per inbox while warming; scale to ~50 once healthy.
- Personalize the first line of #1 with something real (their Google listing, a review count, "no website").

---

## Sequence A — "No website" prospects (your 50 hottest)
These have no site. This is the easiest pitch on the board.

**Email 1 — Day 0**
> **Subject:** {{business}} + a website
>
> Hi {{owner}} — I build websites for fence companies here in {{city}}.
>
> I looked for {{business}} online and couldn't find a real website — just a Google listing. When someone searches "fence company {{city}}," the guys with a real site get the call, even if your work is better.
>
> I can build you one that turns those searches into quote requests. Want me to put together a quick concept so you can see it? No cost.
>
> — Tal

**Email 2 — Day 3**
> **Subject:** re: {{business}} + a website
>
> Following up, {{owner}}. Here's the kind of site I build for fence pros: stackwrk.com/demos/apex-fence — instant quote form, reviews, financing.
>
> Yours would have your name, your photos, your number. Want a free mockup?
>
> — Tal

**Email 3 — Day 7 (breakup)**
> **Subject:** should I close your file?
>
> Not trying to be a pest, {{owner}}. If a website isn't a priority, no problem and I'll stop.
>
> If it is — reply "mockup" and I'll build you a free homepage from your own photos. Takes me a day.
>
> — Tal

## Sequence B — "Has a website but it's weak" prospects
Lead with the specific flaw. Look at their site for 10 seconds first.

**Email 1 — Day 0**
> **Subject:** quick note on {{business}}'s website
>
> Hi {{owner}} — I build websites for fence companies in {{city}}.
>
> Took a look at your site — `[the specific gap: "it's not mobile-friendly" / "there's no quote form" / "it loads really slow on my phone"]`. That's costing you jobs, because homeowners bounce and call the next fence company.
>
> I rebuilt your homepage as a quick concept so you can see the difference. Want the 30-second preview?
>
> — Tal

**Email 2 — Day 3**
> **Subject:** re: quick note on {{business}}'s website
>
> {{owner}} — here's a live example of what I mean: stackwrk.com/demos/apex-fence. Instant quote form, reviews up top, fast on mobile.
>
> Happy to do the same for {{business}} — free mockup, no obligation. Worth a look?
>
> — Tal

**Email 3 — Day 7 (breakup)**
> **Subject:** last one, promise
>
> I'll stop after this, {{owner}}. If getting more quote requests from your website matters this year, reply "mockup" and I'll build you one free from your own photos. If not, no worries — good luck this season.
>
> — Tal

## After they reply "yes / mockup"
1. Build the mockup: duplicate the Apex demo, swap the name + their real photos + phone. ~30–45 min.
2. Record a 60-sec Loom walking through it.
3. Send: *"Here's {{business}}'s new homepage — [Loom link]. If you like it I can have the full thing live in ~10 business days. Got 10 min this week to walk through it?"*
4. On the call: show it, quote all 3 tiers, take the deposit. Send the contract (playbook/03).

## Subject-line bank (A/B test these)
- `{{business}} + a website`
- `quick idea for {{business}}`
- `fence jobs slipping through?`
- `saw {{business}} on Google`
- `{{city}} fence company websites`

## Deliverability checklist (do this BEFORE sending #1)
- [ ] Buy a lookalike domain (e.g. `getstackwrk.com` / `stackwrkhq.com`).
- [ ] Set up SPF, DKIM, DMARC records.
- [ ] Create 1–2 inboxes on it; set a real signature + physical address (CAN-SPAM).
- [ ] Turn on warmup in Instantly/Smartlead; wait 2–3 weeks.
- [ ] Keep bounce rate < 3% (verify emails with MillionVerifier first).
- [ ] Always include a real opt-out line ("Reply STOP and I'll remove you.").
